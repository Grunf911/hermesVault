# AlanNotion Weekly Execution Checklist

## Purpose
A simple weekly checklist for executing AlanNotion promotion with low friction and clear reuse.

## Weekly objective
Pick one paid product, pair it with the best matching free lead magnet, and reuse the same idea across channels.

## Checklist

### 1) Pick the weekly paid product
Choose one paid offer to focus on this week:
- Goals Projects & Tasks
- Automated Sales OS
- Job Seeker Dashboard - Application & Goal Tracker
- Or another paid AlanNotion product

### 2) Pick the matching free lead magnet
Choose the free item that best supports the paid offer.
Examples:
- Goals Projects & Tasks → Simple Projects & Tasks Tracker or Ultimate Coffee & Habit Tracker
- Automated Sales OS → Sales Tracker
- Job Seeker Dashboard → Simple CV or Job Seeker - Application Tracker
- YouTube-focused offers → YouTube Thumbnail Master Class, YouTube Title Master Class, YouTube Video Idea Master Class, or related checklists

### 3) Draft one core message
Write one main idea for the week:
- What problem does this solve?
- Why does the free item help?
- Why is the paid offer the better upgrade?

### 4) Create platform drafts
Prepare drafts for:
- YouTube video or community post
- X post or thread
- Gumroad copy improvement
- Optional support post or short-form repurpose

### 5) Ask for approval before publishing
Do not publish anything public until Alan approves the draft.

### 6) Execute the approved content
After approval:
- publish the content
- update the relevant store page if needed
- note what was published and where

### 7) Log results and findings in the daily journal
After the execution run, record:
- what was published
- what was approved or rejected
- clicks, saves, replies, or sales if available
- what worked
- what did not work
- what should change next week

## Channel order of use
Recommended order:
1. YouTube for authority and explanation
2. X for repurposed visibility
3. Gumroad for conversion
4. Obsidian for internal tracking

## Weekly rule
One core idea should become multiple assets. Do not invent a new strategy every week.
