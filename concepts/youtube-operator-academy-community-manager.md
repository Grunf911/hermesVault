## Newsletter cadence from live posts
The Academy uses weekly update posts to show progress and announce what shipped.

Observed examples:
- Week 9 — classroom being built; ideation and packaging complete; hooks outline in progress
- Week 10 — classroom in progress; first video workflow underway; hooks work ongoing
- Week 11 — first video course nearly complete; weekly Q&A calls planned
- Week 12 — "Your First Video in 3 Days" complete; hooks masterclass nearing release
- Week 13 — hooks masterclass live; join questionnaire work ongoing; first 2 weeks on YouTube course planned

## Community manager operating rules
- Keep weekly updates short, clear, and progress-oriented.
- Reuse the Academy's language: clarity, focus, progress, no overwhelm.
- Highlight what shipped, what is ongoing, and what is next.
- Encourage comments, questions, and visible action.
- Avoid hype, guru language, and long theoretical posts.
- Treat unfinished classroom sections as work-in-progress, not failures.

## Suggested weekly newsletter structure
1. What shipped this week
2. What is currently in progress
3. What is planned next
4. One community prompt or question
5. One reminder about the next step for members
