# GrunfWorks

## Overview
GrunfWorks is Alan Graf's long-term gaming creator brand. Productivity is a support system for the creator business, not the brand itself.

Channel homepage: https://youtube.com/@grunfworks

## Core niche
- Space games
- Engineering and simulation
- Base-building and survival systems

## Content strategy
- Core formats: Let's Plays, tutorials, tips, reviews, previews, analysis
- Style: calm, analytical, tactical, step-by-step
- Bias: long-term episodic Let's Plays, up to ~20 episodes
- Preference: evergreen systems explanations
- Avoid: clickbait, yelling, drama, trend chasing, patch-only coverage unless it unlocks new systems

## Audience
- "Gaming dads"
- Roughly 35-55, mostly male, often married with kids
- Limited but intentional gaming time
- Systems-oriented, depth over hype, likes space, factory, simulation, and survival games
- Desired viewer outcome: calm confidence, clear understanding, feeling caught up without stress

## Products and tools
- YouTuber's Tag Manager (free): lightweight tag store by game/topic, lead generator
- YouTube Operator Template (paid, about $35): end-to-end publishing workflow helper
- Notion tools are helpers, not part of the GrunfWorks brand

## Monetization
- Priority: digital products, community, sponsorships, ads, courses
- Goal: side income that compounds into long-term assets
- Style: free → paid → higher-ticket later
- Sell by demonstrating usage
- Avoid aggressive CTAs, artificial urgency, and hard selling

## Constraints
- Weekly capacity: about 14-17.5 hours total
- Momentum killers: context switching, lack of feedback, limited time, competing life priorities
- Accelerators: checklists, templates, automation, time-boxing

## AI collaboration rules
- Give direct instructions with strategic context
- Use step-by-step execution for complex tasks
- Challenge ideas when they do not make sense
- Default to action
- Tone: brutally honest by default, analytical when clarifying, creative when ideating

## 3-year vision
- Ads: about $300-$500/month
- Digital products: about $2,000-$3,000/month
- Audience: 30,000-50,000 subscribers
- Lifestyle: buy back time through automation without increasing workload
- Reputation: space games, systems/mechanics explanations, calm long-form Let's Plays

## Absolute rules
- Prefer evergreen content over trends
- Never increase workload without clear ROI
- Always assume limited uninterrupted time
